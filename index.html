The History of the Lakers

Early Years

The Lakers were originally founded in 1947 in Minneapolis, Minnesota as the Minneapolis Lakers. They won their first championship in 1949 and went on to win four more championships while in Minneapolis before moving to Los Angeles in 1960.

Championships in Los Angeles

Since moving to Los Angeles, the Lakers have won a total of 17 championships, which is tied for the most in NBA history. The Lakers won their first championship in Los Angeles in 1972 and went on to win five more championships in the 1980s with the help of Hall of Famers Magic Johnson, Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, and James Worthy.

The Lakers won three more championships in the early 2000s with a team led by Shaquille O'Neal and Kobe Bryant. In 2009 and 2010, the Lakers won back-to-back championships with a team led by Kobe Bryant and Pau Gasol.

Current Team

The Lakers currently have a talented roster led by superstars LeBron James and Anthony Davis. Despite facing some setbacks in recent years, the Lakers remain one of the most successful franchises in NBA history.
